Factors Influencing Migration to Cloud Computing

Abstract


Cloud Computing is the way of delivering computing resources, storage resources and other infrastructure on demand and charged using the pay-as-you-go model. In recent times, small, medium and large enterprises are moving towards cloud computing mainly because of the economic benefit by cutting the cost of existing infrastructure (Date et al. 2015). Extensive research is going on the technology itself and many studies focused on finding issues related to business. Among them, many studies focused on opportunities and risks involved in migration to cloud computing but not the factors that influence the migration (Date et al. 2015). The primary objective of this research paper is to develop a conceptual model showing the factors that influence the migration of the enterprises to cloud computing. The research question addressed in this paper is to identify the factors that influence the intention of small, and medium enterprises (SMEs’) to adopt cloud computing. We address the above question by recognizing the factors considered before migration. A conceptual model combining the factors of Technology Organization and Environment – Technology Acceptance Model – Diffusion of Innovation (TOE-TAM-DOI) frameworks is developed which contributes to the enterprises while deciding to adopt cloud computing.
